Cookies Policy

This Cookies Policy explains how woodHandcraft ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website, woodhandcraft.com (the "Site"). By continuing to browse or use our Site, you are agreeing to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree to our use of cookies, you should set your browser settings accordingly or not use our Site.

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information. Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your personal computer or mobile device when you go offline, while session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser.

1. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several reasons, including:

  • Essential Website Functionality: To enable core features like secure login, shopping cart functionality, and remembering your preferences. Without these, the site would not function correctly.
  • Website Performance and Analytics: To understand how you interact with our Site, which pages are most popular, and to identify areas for improvement. This helps us enhance your browsing experience.
  • Personalization: To remember your choices (like language or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features.
  • Advertising and Marketing: To deliver relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1. Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our Site. They enable you to navigate the website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the Site and adding items to your shopping cart. Without these cookies, services like online shopping or e-billing cannot be provided. They are typically set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. You cannot opt-out of these cookies as they are fundamental to the Site's functionality. Blocking these cookies will likely result in the Site not performing as expected. They do not store any personally identifiable information.

Examples of necessary cookies include:

  • Session Cookies: Used to maintain your session as you navigate the website, ensuring that you remain logged in and your shopping cart contents are remembered. These are temporary and expire when you close your browser.
  • Security Cookies: Help to identify and prevent security risks. For example, they may detect multiple failed login attempts to protect your account from brute-force attacks.
  • Load Balancing Cookies: Distribute network traffic across multiple servers to ensure the website remains responsive and available, even under heavy load.
  • Consent Cookies: Record your cookie preferences, ensuring that your choices are respected on subsequent visits.
  • User Input Cookies: Used to track user input, such as when filling out forms or adding items to a shopping cart.
  • Authentication Cookies: Identify you as a logged-in user and enable you to access restricted areas of the site.
  • Shopping Cart Cookies: Store information about the items you've added to your shopping cart, allowing you to proceed with your purchase.
  • Language Preference Cookies: Remember your preferred language settings for the website.
  • Currency Preference Cookies: Store your chosen currency for displaying prices.
  • Fraud Prevention Cookies: Help detect and prevent fraudulent activities on the website, such as suspicious transactions.

2.2. Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our Site, such as which pages visitors go to most often, and if they get error messages from web pages. These cookies do not collect information that identifies a visitor. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. It is only used to improve how a website works. By understanding user behavior, we can optimize content, streamline navigation, and enhance overall user satisfaction. These cookies are crucial for us to make data-driven decisions about site improvements.

Examples of performance and analytics cookies include:

  • Google Analytics Cookies: Used to collect information about how visitors use our Site. We use the information to compile reports and to help us improve the Site. These cookies collect information in an anonymous form, including the number of visitors to the Site, where visitors have come to the Site from, and the pages they visited.
  • Heatmap and Session Recording Cookies: Tools that visually represent user interaction on pages, showing where users click, scroll, and spend time, helping us identify usability issues.
  • A/B Testing Cookies: Used to test different versions of a webpage to see which performs better in terms of user engagement or conversion.
  • Content Popularity Cookies: Track which articles, products, or sections of the site are most frequently viewed, informing content strategy.
  • Site Speed Optimization Cookies: Help measure and improve the loading speed of various elements on the website.
  • Error Tracking Cookies: Log errors or broken links encountered by users, assisting in quick resolution and site maintenance.
  • Device Information Cookies: Collect anonymous data about the type of device (desktop, mobile, tablet) and browser used to access the site, helping optimize responsiveness.
  • Referral Source Cookies: Track how users arrive at our site (e.g., from a search engine, social media, or another website), informing marketing efforts.
  • Conversion Tracking Cookies: Measure the effectiveness of specific calls to action or marketing campaigns by tracking user actions like purchases or sign-ups.
  • User Flow Analysis Cookies: Provide insights into the typical paths users take through the website, identifying potential bottlenecks or areas for improved navigation.

2.3. Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow the Site to remember choices you make (such as your user name, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features. For instance, a website may be able to provide you with local weather reports or traffic news by storing in a cookie the region in which you are currently located. These cookies can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages that you can customize. They may also be used to provide services you have asked for, such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites.

Examples of functionality cookies include:

  • Remember Me Cookies: Allow you to stay logged in on subsequent visits, saving you the hassle of re-entering credentials.
  • Personalized Content Cookies: Deliver content tailored to your past interactions or preferences, such as recommending products based on your browsing history.
  • Chat Support Cookies: Enable live chat functionality and remember your chat history for a seamless support experience.
  • Video Player Preference Cookies: Store your preferred video quality settings or playback volume.
  • Social Media Sharing Cookies: Facilitate easy sharing of content from our Site to your social media platforms.
  • Accessibility Preference Cookies: Remember your accessibility settings, such as high contrast mode or larger font sizes.
  • Recently Viewed Items Cookies: Display products you've recently looked at for easy recall.
  • Wishlist Cookies: Store items you've added to a wishlist, even if you're not logged in.
  • Geographic Location Cookies: Provide localized content or currency based on your detected location.
  • Form Auto-fill Cookies: Remember information you've previously entered into forms (e.g., name, address) to speed up future submissions.

2.4. Targeting and Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of the advertising campaign. They are usually placed by advertising networks with the website operatorâ€™s permission.   • Ad Campaign Performance Cookies: Measure how many clicks an ad receives and how many conversions result from it.
  • Demographic Targeting Cookies: Collect anonymous demographic data to help advertisers target ads more effectively.
  • Social Media Advertising Cookies: Used by social media platforms to deliver targeted ads to you based on your interactions with our content on their platforms.
  • Cross-Site Tracking Cookies: Follow your browsing activity across different websites to build a profile of your interests for advertising purposes.
  • Ad Frequency Capping Cookies: Prevent you from seeing the same advertisement too many times.
  • Ad Attribution Cookies: Help us understand which marketing channels (e.g., email, social media, search) contribute to sales or leads.
